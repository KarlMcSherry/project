Living Costs

Living Costs in Dublin can be comparitively much more expensive than other similarly sized cities.

Groceries

Groceries in Dublin are often of reasonable price compared to the quality. Dublin has 4 popular supermarkets.

  • Tesco, which stocks a range of brand name and own brand items.
  • Dunnes Stores, which is similar to Tesco, however, places emphasis on Irish products, and is slightly more expensive.
  • Lidl, which stocks primarily own brand items, which are of high quality.
  • Aldi, which also stocks primarily high quality own brand items, however, offers a different selection from Lidl.

Bills

Bills in Ireland can be quite expensive compared to other cities.

  • Mobile phone bill is likely to cost somewhere around an average of €40 a month.
  • Electricity for one person is likely to cost an average of €15, depending on useage.
  • In winter months, heating will usually cost about €15 per week for the average household.
  • High speed internet is quite cheap in Ireland, with packages starting at €30 per month.

Eating Out

Eating out in Dublin can be either quite expensive, or very reasonable, depending on your tastes.

  • Casual restaurants will usually cost around €20 per person for a meal including a drink./li>
  • Formal restaurants can be quite expensive, with a bill in excess of €50 per person not being uncommon.
  • Many places around the city offer very reasonable deals on lunch, with a nearly endless amount of lunch offers for €10 or under.
  • Many supermakets offer lunch meal deals for under €5, which will usually have a salad/sandwich, a snack and a drink.
